1. What is a Seedball?

Seed balls, also known as earth balls, consist of a variety of different seeds rolled within a ball of clay, preferably volcanic pyroclastic red clay. Various additives may be included, such as humus or compost. These are placed around the seeds, at the center of the ball, to provide microbial inoculants. Cotton-fibres or liquefied paper are sometimes mixed into the clay in order to strengthen it, or liquefied paper mash coated on the outside to further protect the clay ball during sowing by throwing, or in particularly harsh habitats.

2.How are we planting our trees?

As we are using Seed balls which require no water, we are planting them by throwing them from the drone to the areas where it is not convenient to reach.

3.How do we monitor our plants?

Our team visited the area where they throw seeds and monitor through the drone again.

4.Are we a non-profit org?

Yes! We are absolutely a non-profit organization. We operate through donation grants for the sake of the environment.

5.Where can we find a seedball?

The seedballs can be found in the shop section of the website. If you order it will be delivered to your place.

6.How can we donate seedball?

If you don't have time to plant trees but have a good heart for the environment you can donate seedballs as well. Currently there are three methods for payment on the donate section of our website.

7.Can I work here as a volunteer?

Yes we need good numbers of dedicated volunteer teams. Just show your interest by filling this form and we will reach you. Happy plantation.

8.I have no drone, how can I plant seedballs ?

It doesn't matter if you don't have a drone. You can use a catapult for throwing seeds to the field.
